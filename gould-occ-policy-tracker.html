Comptroller Jonathan V. Gould — Policy Action Tracker

32nd Comptroller of the Currency  ·  Confirmed July 10, 2025 (50–45)  ·  Sworn in July 15, 2025

Gould has moved quickly since confirmation to reset the OCC's supervisory posture — reducing regulatory burden on community banks, defending and expanding National Bank Act preemption authority, and opening the door to fintech and digital-asset charters under the GENIUS Act framework. This tracker organizes his major actions across three policy pillars. Each entry links to the underlying OCC release, bulletin, or Federal Register notice where available.

Status

Gould has made reducing regulatory burden on community banks a centerpiece of his tenure — targeting examination frequency, BSA/AML compliance costs, CRA burden, capital requirements, and the politically charged use of "reputation risk" in supervision.

A central Gould priority has been defending — and proactively expanding — National Bank Act preemption authority following adverse First and Ninth Circuit rulings on state escrow-interest laws. His approach combines formal rulemaking, amicus engagement, and coalition-building, and has now produced finalized escrow rules and a favorable Second Circuit ruling on remand from Cantero.

Gould has cleared the path for non-traditional and fintech bank charters at a pace not seen in recent years — granting conditional approval to Erebor Bank (the first de novo national bank charter of his tenure) and positioning the OCC as the primary federal regulator for stablecoin issuers under the GENIUS Act.
