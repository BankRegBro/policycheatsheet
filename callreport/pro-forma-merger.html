Pro forma merger model

Combine two balance sheets_

Pick an acquirer and a target, set the deal terms, and the model builds a real purchase-accounting combination: goodwill and core deposit intangible, fair-value marks on loans and securities, cost saves, the CECL day-two provision, and a recomputed capital stack. The output is the line that matters in a board memo, what the deal does to ROA, ROE, ROTCE, the efficiency ratio, capital ratios, and tangible book value. Everything recomputes as you move a lever. Seed values pull from FDIC data and every input is editable, so you can run a real bank or your own numbers.

Source: FDIC BankFind Suite API via Cloudflare Worker proxy. Balances are period-end; income statement items are year-to-date and annualized using the report month. The provision line is seeded as a residual so each bank's reconstructed earnings tie to its reported return on assets. This is a screening and teaching model, not a fairness opinion. It applies the acquisition method (ASC 805) in a deliberately simplified form: deferred taxes are applied at a single entity rate, core deposit intangible amortizes straight-line, the CECL day-two provision is modeled as a one-time charge on the non-PCD share of the credit mark, rate and securities marks accrete back to interest income straight-line, and risk-weighted assets are scaled from reported capital ratios rather than rebuilt loan by loan. It assumes no preferred stock, an AOCI opt-out, and that cash consideration is funded as selected: from the balance sheet (giving up the acquirer's net earning-asset yield), from new subordinated debt counted as Tier 2, or from a new common equity raise added to CET1. An equity raise is modeled in aggregate, not per share. Real deals also turn on antitrust and deposit concentration, CRA and approval risk, customer attrition, contract and severance detail, and the rate environment at close.
Educational tool. Not investment-banking, accounting, legal, or fairness-opinion advice.
