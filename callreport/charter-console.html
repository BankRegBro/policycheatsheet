Charter application prep

Draft a national bank charter_

Set the charter type and structure, build the organizing group, size the capital plan, and the console assembles a populated first draft of the OCC charter application: the cover and overview, a business plan scaffold built to the Business Plan Guidelines, the standard de novo conditions tuned to your capital, the minimum-policy commitments, the biographical-report roster, and a phase timeline with the statutory deadlines. Every section branches on whether you are organizing a full-service national bank or a national trust bank. The output is a working draft and a readiness read, not legal advice.

Sources: National Bank Act, 12 USC 21 et seq. and 12 USC 27(a); 12 CFR 5.20, 5.26, and 12 CFR 9; OCC Comptroller's Licensing Manual, Charters booklet (Dec. 2021) and Background Investigations booklet (Feb. 2025); the Interagency Charter and Federal Deposit Insurance Application (FDIC 6200/05) and Interagency Biographical and Financial Report (FDIC 6200/06); OCC Bulletin 2007-21 (trust-bank capital and liquidity); and the OCC's March 2026 national bank chartering final rule. This console produces a structured first draft and a completeness read. It does not file with the OCC, run background checks, build the full financial projection model, or replace counsel. Capital floors, the Tier 1 leverage minimum, and condition durations are set case by case in the OCC's decision letter and are shown here as planning defaults, not commitments.
