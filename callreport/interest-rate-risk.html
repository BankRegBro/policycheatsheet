Interest rate risk screen

Shock the balance sheet_

Lay out the bank's repricing and maturity ladder the way the Call Report does, set the behavioral assumptions that never appear in any filing (deposit betas, the effective duration of core deposits, prepayment behavior), and the screen runs both lenses of rate risk: earnings at risk, the change in net interest income, and economic value at risk, the change in EVE. It shocks the curve with parallel moves and the six Basel scenarios, applies the supervisory outlier test, and shows the AFS and HTM capital lens. Loaded with an example bank, and every cell is yours to overwrite with your own numbers.

Inputs
Method: a gap-and-duration screen built on the public Call Report repricing ladder (Schedules RC-B, RC-C, and RC-E) plus the behavioral assumptions a bank holds internally. Earnings at risk reprices balances inside a twelve-month window at the fraction of the year they stay outstanding after repricing, with non-maturity deposits repricing at an asymmetric up and down beta. Economic value at risk applies bucket modified durations in a dollar-duration framework; the six Basel scenarios use the USD standardized shock sizes and the e^(-t/4) rotation weighting; the supervisory outlier test flags a worst-case EVE decline beyond 15% of Tier 1 capital. It is a screening and teaching model, not an option-adjusted ALM system: it does not price the full convexity of mortgage cash flows, basis risk across indices, or embedded caps and floors, it treats core-deposit life as an input rather than a model, and it assumes reinvestment at the shocked curve. Results are only as good as the betas, durations, and ladder you enter.
Educational tool. Not investment, accounting, or regulatory advice.
