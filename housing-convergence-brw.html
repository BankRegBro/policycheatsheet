The Housing Convergence

The conclusion, up front

Supply-side and deregulatory, by statute and by rule, all at once

The four documents are not separate stories. They are coordinated moves in one strategy: expand housing supply and cut compliance cost rather than subsidize demand. The clearest convergence is manufactured and factory-built housing, which appears in three of the four instruments at once. The most consequential survivor of reconciliation is the community-banking title, which had no Senate counterpart yet made it into the final bill intact. The bill is finished in form and awaits signature; the executive actions are open for comment right now.

Analyst notes

How to read the package

Strip away the press releases and four forces explain the whole move.

A note on what is not here. This dispatch reads the four documents as one strategy. It is not a section-by-section summary of the 374-page bill, and the rules remain proposals that can change through comment. The controlling text for the legislation is H.R. 6644 EAS2; the controlling text for each rule is its Federal Register entry.

About this dispatch

This file reads four converging housing actions of June 2026 against their primary sources: the 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act (H.R. 6644), HUD's manufactured-home and FHA streamlining actions, and FHFA's Duty to Serve rewrite. Conclusions are front-loaded; provisions are cited to title, section, or Federal Register page.

The legislation has passed both chambers and awaits the President's signature, so it is not yet law. The rules are proposals open to change through notice and comment. Status and exact text should be confirmed against the sources before any provision is relied upon.
